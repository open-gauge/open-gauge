## What is Open Gauge?

**Open Gauge** is a self-hosted platform for managing industrial
sensors, instrumentation assets, calibration records, calibration coefficients, certificates,
and traceability data. It's built for laboratories, industrial sites, and engineering
organizations that need to know — with evidence — that a measurement can be trusted.

Open Gauge keeps track of:

- The **asset registry**: every sensor and data-acquisition (DAQ) device you own, its
  specifications, and where it's physically located.
- **Calibration history**: every calibration event, as an immutable, append-only record —
  never overwritten, never silently corrected.
- **Measurement uncertainty**, computed per [JCGM 100:2008 (GUM)](./reference/standards-glossary.mdx)
  and reported per [ISO/IEC 17025:2017](./reference/standards-glossary.mdx).
- **Certificates**, procedures, sites/locations, and an audit trail of who changed what and
  when.

## How Open Gauge is organized

Open Gauge models a simple hierarchy:

```
Organization
 └─ Locations (site → building → laboratory → ...)
     └─ Assets (sensors, DAQs)
         └─ Sensor channels (one per measured physical quantity)
             └─ Calibrations (immutable history, one row per event)
```
